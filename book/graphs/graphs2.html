Graph Algorithms

Behind each complex system there is an intricate network that encodes the interactions between the system’s components. A key discovery of network science is that the architecture of networks emerging in various domains of science, nature, and technology are similar to each other, a consequence of being governed by the same organizing principles. Consequently we can use a common set of mathematical tools to explore these systems [Barabási, 2016]

  • Graphs are universal approximators. They are interdiscipilinary. Even though the fields are different it can be the formulation can be generalised and the same concepts can be used in every discipline.

  • Some of the most succeful companies base there business models on networks. Eg: facebook, twitter, google etc.

  • A mapping of genes, proteins and cellular components as molecular networks aims at understanding behaviour of cellular networks and human disease. Harvard started a separate division called network medicine.

  • Network pharmacology is a stream dedicated to drug discovery.

  • Epidemics are transportation networks. These can be predicted and forecasted.

  • Neuroscience. A mapping of the brain neurons helps us understand how the brain works and find cure for neurological diseases.

  • Organizational networks help identify the most influential people in the organization there by making them the point of contact to spread information about goals of the higher management.

fig = plt.figure(figsize=(12, 3))

    ax.set_yticks([])

  • Cascading failures (Vulnerability due to interconnectivity) : Electricity grids and pandemic networks are a good example for cascading failures. Due to the interconnectivity and interdependency, a failure/defect in one node at a given point of time \(t\) results in the failure of the neighbouring/adjacent nodes. This eventually spreads throughout the network.
